National Parks

Great Smoky Mountains

  • 10,099,276 visits in 2014
  • The wispy, smoke-like fog that hangs over the Smoky Mountains comes from rain and evaporation from trees.
  • Approximately 1,500 black bears live in the park (this means there's an average of two bears per square mile)
  • Home to at least 30 different species of salamanders

Grand Canyon

  • 4,756,771 visits in 2014
  • From Yavapai Point on the South Rim of the Grand Canyon, the drop to the Colorado River below is 4,600 feet.
  • The South Rim is open 365 days a year, but the North Rim is only open from May - October.
  • At the bottom, where Unkar Creek joins the Colorado River sits Unkar Delta where prehistoric Pueblo people occupied numerous sites here for about 350 years.

Yosemite

  • 3,882,642 visits in 2014
  • Yosemite Falls is fed mostly by snowmelt - peak flow usually happens in late May, but by August, Yosemite Falls is often dry.
  • Seven present-day tribes descend from the people who first called this area home.
  • Yosemite supports more than 400 species of vertebrates, including fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals.

FAQ

Q1. How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Approximately 22,000 diverse professionals and 221,000 volunteers.
Q2. How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
Q3. What is the largest national park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres
Q4. What is the smallest national park site?
Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres.
Q5. How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2014: 292,800,082
Q6. What is the most-visited national park?
Great Smoky Mountains with 10,099,276 visits in 2014
Q7. What is the second most-visted national park?
Grand Canyon with 4,756,771 visits in 2014
Q8. What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior.
Q9. Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Jonathan B. Jarvis
Q10. How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 407 areas covering more than 84 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
